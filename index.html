About Me

Hi there! I'm a PhD student in Learning Sciences & Psychological Studies at UNC Chapel Hill, working at the intersection of self-regulated learning, large language models, and causal inference methods for education research. I'm also pursuing an M.S. in Applied Data Science alongside my doctoral work.

My research asks how students regulate their learning, how AI can help us understand and support that process at scale, and how rigorous causal and statistical methods can uncover for whom and under what conditions interventions actually work. Recent projects include using LLMs to code think-aloud protocols of self-regulated learning, evaluating LLM feedback that supports teacher professional vision, and developing new causal moderation methods for heterogeneous treatment effects.

Outside of research, I enjoy reading science fiction and dancing — both help me stay creative and balanced while pursuing my academic goals.
