Research Focus

Self-Regulated Learning

"Why do the same SRL interventions produce different effects for different learners — and what would it take to match support to the learner who needs it?"

My work on the resource-intervention match framework (Learning and Individual Differences, 2026) explains differential effects of self-regulated learning interventions, moving beyond black-box average effects toward theory that predicts for whom and why interventions work.

LLMs in Education

"Can large language models code think-aloud protocols and generate feedback with the theoretical fidelity that learning science demands?"

I develop and evaluate LLM-based methods for automated coding of SRL think-aloud data (Journal of Learning Analytics) and for generating feedback that supports pre-service teachers' professional vision (LAK '26), alongside an AI-based SRL chatbot designed around autonomy, competence, and relatedness.

Causal Inference Methods

"How can we recover credible moderation effects from observational data when treatments are binary and moderators are continuous?"

I develop double-weighting approaches for estimating causal moderation effects and contribute to practical guidance on moderation analysis for studying how context, identity, and culture shape intervention effects (Journal of School Psychology).

Learning Analytics

"What do sequences of learning behavior over a semester reveal about motivation, engagement, and risk of dropout?"

Using sequence analysis and survival modeling on log and engagement data from college STEM courses, I study how predictive learning sequences and early motivational and performance signals relate to persistence and dropout in introductory science courses.
